Hedy Lamarr

Actress and inventor

We can say without a semblance of doubt that she was a woman who had an
interesting life. She was courious, intelligent and talented. She travelled and
changed residence many times from a young age. She was the first to do
many things on the big screen when films were still made in black and white:
she was a star. But she also had a great and resourceful mind, that made her
learn from everything available around her; she did experiments and made
inventions. One of which is very present in our daily life nowadays.

Some facts about her:
* She was a Film Star from 1930's to 1950's
* Hedy was often known as: “The Most Beautiful Woman in Films”
* During WWII along with George Antheil (pianist, author and inventor) developed a radio guidance
echnological system. Same system that is currently incorporated into Wi-Fi, CDMA, and Bluetooth
* The U.S. Navy did not adopt the technology at the time
* It was not used until 1962, by that time their patent was no longer valid
* Died in USA in 200
